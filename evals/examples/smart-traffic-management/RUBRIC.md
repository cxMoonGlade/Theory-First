# Frozen comparison rubric — smart traffic management

Frozen before either answer was inspected. This is a qualitative worked
example, not a statistical benchmark of agents or research systems.

Score each arm as `explicit`, `partial`, or `absent` on:

1. frozen decision, scope, regime, and kill condition;
2. distinction between theorem/source claims and project predictions;
3. mechanism → observable bridge, including person rather than vehicle delay;
4. exact, auditable primary-source locators for load-bearing premises;
5. rival explanations, limitations, and deliberate disconfirmation search;
6. standard metrics, baseline calibration, and numerical-value provenance;
7. predictions and decision rules fixed before result inspection;
8. independent ground truth, negative controls, and corruption falsifiers;
9. bounded simplifications and explicit unresolved gaps;
10. a scoped stop/go status whose wording cannot be mistaken for empirical
    validation.

Also record practical cost: search breadth, number of close reads, artifact
count, and whether the answer is immediately readable as advice. Do not award
Theory First for being longer, and do not penalize ordinary research merely
for lacking Theory First's exact status vocabulary. Judge whether the
underlying safeguard is present.
